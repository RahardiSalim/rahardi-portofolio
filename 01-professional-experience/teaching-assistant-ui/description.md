# Teaching Assistant - University of Indonesia

## Short Description

Providing instruction across six technical courses including AI & Data Science, Digital Systems, Calculus 2, Statistics, Discrete Mathematics, and Automata Theory while coordinating TA operations for International Class Program.

## Long Description

Serving as a Teaching Assistant at University of Indonesia has been a masterclass in communication, pedagogy, and technical depth. Teaching isn't just about knowing the material—it's about understanding where students struggle and crafting explanations that create genuine comprehension.

My TA responsibilities span six technical courses, each presenting unique pedagogical challenges:

**Artificial Intelligence & Data Science** requires demystifying complex algorithms, making neural networks feel less like black boxes and more like engineered systems with understandable components.

**Digital Systems** demands building intuition about hardware abstractions, helping students bridge the gap between logic gates and the computers they use daily.

**Calculus 2** and **Statistics and Probabilities** involve making mathematical abstractions concrete, showing how integration techniques or probability distributions solve real problems worth caring about.

**Discrete Mathematics I and II** test logical thinking and proof construction—skills that underpin all computer science but often feel disconnected from programming until students see the connections.

**Automata Theory** stretches conceptual thinking, requiring students to reason about computation in formal, mathematical terms.

Beyond course-specific instruction, I coordinate TA operations for the International Class Program in Discrete Mathematics I. This administrative role involves scheduling office hours, coordinating with professors, managing assignment grading workflows, and ensuring consistent academic support across multiple TA sections.

The most challenging and rewarding aspect is diagnosing individual learning gaps. One student might struggle with mathematical notation while understanding concepts perfectly. Another might grasp theory but struggle with implementation. Effective teaching means adapting explanations to meet students where they are.

Office hours become spaces for debugging not just code, but understanding. The goal isn't just helping students pass exams—it's cultivating the problem-solving approaches and technical foundations they'll rely on throughout their careers.

Teaching has sharpened my own understanding immeasurably. Explaining concepts forces precision. Student questions reveal assumptions I hadn't examined. The best teaching is mutual learning, and that reciprocity makes every office hour session worthwhile.
