## Short Description

Gold medal with Most Creative Approach award at IPB University's GammaFest 2025 for building an innovative citation recommendation system leveraging NLP and graph-based approaches for academic paper discovery.

## Long Description

GammaFest 2025 at IPB University challenged participants to reimagine how researchers discover relevant citations—a fundamental task in academic work that often involves tedious manual searching through databases and reference lists. Our team didn't just build a functional system; we built one recognized for creativity, earning both first place and the Most Creative Approach award.

The citation recommendation problem seems straightforward: given a paper draft or research topic, suggest relevant papers to cite. But the challenge is nuanced. Good citation recommendations require understanding research context, identifying seminal works, recognizing emerging trends, and matching technical depth to the author's needs.

We approached this as a multi-modal NLP challenge. Academic papers contain rich signals: titles encode core topics, abstracts summarize contributions, full text reveals methodologies, and citation networks expose intellectual lineages. Our system integrated these diverse signals into coherent recommendations.

The natural language processing pipeline extracted semantic meaning from paper text, identifying key concepts, methodologies, and research questions. But we didn't stop at text analysis. Citation networks form graphs where papers are nodes and citations are edges. This graph structure reveals authority (highly-cited papers), communities (clusters of related work), and intellectual flow (how ideas propagate).

Our creative approach combined NLP-derived semantic similarity with graph-based authority metrics. A paper might be semantically related but obscure, or authoritative but tangentially relevant. Our system balanced these dimensions, surfacing citations that were both topically aligned and impactful.

The judges specifically recognized our creativity—not just in algorithmic approaches but in thinking about user experience. Researchers don't just want paper titles; they need context about why each citation is relevant, how it fits their narrative, and what contribution it makes to the field. Our system provided that contextual guidance.

Winning at a professional-level competition validated our hypothesis that citation recommendation could move beyond simple keyword matching or collaborative filtering. By treating it as a multimodal problem combining NLP, graph analysis, and information retrieval, we built something genuinely useful.

This project reinforced the importance of taking holistic approaches to complex problems. The best solutions often emerge not from applying single cutting-edge techniques, but from creatively combining multiple methods in ways that leverage their complementary strengths.
