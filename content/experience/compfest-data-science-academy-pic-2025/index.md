# Person In Charge, Data Science Academy - COMPFEST 17

## Short Description

Led data science education program with 1.8% acceptance rate, coordinating 23 industry experts and creating technical assessments for 30 selected participants from 1,700+ applicants.

## Long Description

Leading the Data Science Academy at COMPFEST 17 was an exercise in building education infrastructure at scale. With over 1,700 applicants competing for just 30 spots—a 1.8% acceptance rate more selective than many universities—the challenge was creating a program worthy of that selectivity.

The vision was clear: build a comprehensive data science curriculum that bridges academic theory with industry practice. But vision without execution is just aspiration. I designed the end-to-end academy operations, from application screening through final project evaluations.

The curriculum balanced breadth and depth. We created technical assessments that tested not just coding skills but analytical thinking and problem-solving approaches. Kaggle challenges became our practical proving ground, building skills in both prediction modeling and data analysis through real-world scenarios: electricity consumption forecasting and financial complaints classification.

But perhaps the most impactful work was building partnerships. I secured collaborations with three industry leaders—Data Science Indonesia, Mekari, and Pusilkom UI—creating a network of 23 experts comprising 10 speakers, 10 mentors, and 3 judges. These weren't token partnerships; each expert brought specific domain expertise that enriched the learning experience.

The speakers delivered workshops on cutting-edge techniques. The mentors provided one-on-one guidance as participants wrestled with real problems. The judges evaluated final projects with the rigor of production code reviews, teaching participants what industry-grade work actually looks like.

Managing this operation meant coordinating schedules, aligning expectations, curating content, and ensuring every interaction added value. It meant making hard decisions about curriculum priorities and being accountable for 30 participants' learning outcomes.

The real measure of success wasn't the acceptance rate—it was watching participants grow from students with potential into practitioners with practical skills, ready to contribute to Indonesia's growing data science ecosystem. That transformation made every coordination challenge worthwhile.
