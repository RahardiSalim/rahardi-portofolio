## Long Description

The Natural Language Processing Specialization by DeepLearning.AI provides a rigorous, from-scratch journey through the full stack of modern NLP — from classical probabilistic methods to cutting-edge transformer architectures.

NLP with Classification and Vector Spaces begins with sentiment analysis using logistic regression and naïve Bayes, then moves to word vector representations. Building word embeddings from scratch and using cosine similarity, PCA, and locality-sensitive hashing for nearest-neighbor search creates foundational intuition about semantic meaning in high-dimensional space.

NLP with Probabilistic Models covers the probabilistic foundations underpinning language: autocorrect via minimum edit distance, Part-of-Speech tagging with hidden Markov models and Viterbi decoding, N-gram language models, and Word2Vec training. These classical algorithms remain relevant in hybrid systems and for understanding what neural models have replaced.

NLP with Sequence Models transitions to neural approaches: dense and recurrent networks for sentiment analysis and named entity recognition, LSTMs for text generation, and Siamese networks for question duplicate detection. The Trax framework provides hands-on experience with modern deep learning libraries.

NLP with Attention Models explores the attention mechanism and transformer architecture — the foundation of BERT, GPT, and every major contemporary language model. Neural machine translation, text summarization, question answering, and chatbot construction demonstrate the breadth of what transformers enable.

This specialization directly informs work on citation systems, phishing detection, and RAG-based applications — providing the theoretical depth to understand not just how to use NLP tools, but why they work.
