# AI Research Assistant at ETH Zürich

## Short Description

Accelerated egocentric video research by curating high-precision dataset of 120+ videos with sub-second temporal accuracy for complex human-object interaction sequences.

## Long Description

Working remotely as an AI Research Assistant at ETH Zürich represented a significant step into academic research rigor. The project centered on advancing egocentric video understanding—teaching machines to comprehend human activities from a first-person perspective, a critical capability for applications ranging from augmented reality to assistive robotics.

My primary contribution was data curation, but this wasn't simply collecting videos. Egocentric video presents unique challenges: rapid camera movements, partial object visibility, complex hand-object interactions, and temporal dependencies that span seconds or minutes. Each annotation required precision.

I curated a high-precision dataset of over 120 videos, ensuring sub-second temporal accuracy across complex human-object interaction sequences. This meant meticulously labeling when objects appeared, how they were manipulated, and how actions transitioned—details that became training signals for deep learning models.

The impact was tangible: accelerated model training cycles. Clean, precisely annotated data meant researchers could iterate faster, testing hypotheses about model architectures and learning strategies without getting bogged down in noisy labels or ambiguous ground truth.

Working in a remote capacity with a world-class research institution taught me the discipline of academic research: systematic methodology, rigorous quality standards, and documentation practices that enable reproducibility. The experience bridged my applied AI work in industry with the foundational research that pushes the field forward.

This position reinforced a key insight: in machine learning, the quality of your data often matters more than the sophistication of your model. Careful curation, precise labeling, and thoughtful dataset construction create the foundation upon which all subsequent research builds.
